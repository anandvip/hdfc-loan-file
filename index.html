Farm Loan Increase Request - Vipul Anand

Introduction

Dear Kartik and Mr. Jagjit (Branch Manager, HDFC Bank, Railway Road, Kurukshetra),

I hope this proposal finds you well. I'm writing to formally request an increase in my farm loan limit to support the implementation of innovative agricultural technologies.

Current Situation and Proposed Change

  • Current loan limit: ₹1,000,000 for 5 acres (40 kanals)
  • Current bank valuation: ₹25,000 per kanal (₹200,000 per acre)
  • Actual market value (2024): ₹6,500,000 per acre (₹812,500 per kanal)
  • Proposed new limit: ₹2,000,000
  • Proposed bank valuation: ₹50,000 per kanal (₹400,000 per acre)

Justification for Loan Increase

  1. Significant Undervaluation: The current bank valuation is only 3% of the actual market value, providing a substantial safety margin.
  2. Massive Collateral Coverage: The mortgaged land's market value (₹32,500,000) provides 16 times coverage for the requested loan amount.
  3. Low Loan-to-Value (LTV) Ratio: The proposed LTV of 6.15% is far below typical agricultural loan ratios of 50-85%.
  4. Risk Mitigation: The large gap between loan amount and collateral value offers exceptional protection against market fluctuations.
  5. Economic Opportunity: The increased loan will enable substantial investments in farm productivity, potentially leading to increased yields and profitability.

Planned Use of Funds

The increased loan will be used to implement Israeli agricultural technologies, including:

  • Drip irrigation systems
  • Polyhouse farming structures
  • Advanced fertigation systems

These technologies are expected to significantly improve yield and resource efficiency. The estimated setup cost is approximately ₹50,000-70,000 per kanal.

Vision and Goals

My initiative aims to create sustainable alternatives to traditional farming methods. Key aspects include:

  • Crop diversification
  • Implementation of water-efficient technologies
  • Promotion of sustainable farming practices
  • Creating a model farm for education and inspiration

Insurance Considerations

I'm interested in comprehensive coverage for vegetable crops typically covered in Haryana, including tomatoes, eggplants, cabbage, cauliflower, and others. I would appreciate guidance on suitable insurance options for these planned activities.

Bank Relationship

Our relationship dates back to 2005 in Bengaluru. This long-standing association demonstrates our mutual trust and commitment. I believe this loan increase will further strengthen our partnership.

Conclusion

This loan increase represents a low-risk opportunity to support innovative and sustainable farming practices. I'm prepared to provide any additional documentation or projections you may require.

Thank you for your consideration. I look forward to discussing this proposal further.

Sincerely,
Vipul Anand
Engineer & Farmer
